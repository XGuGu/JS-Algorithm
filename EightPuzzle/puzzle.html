The Problem

The 8 puzzle problem is a puzzle invented and popularized by Noyes Palmer Chapman in the 1870s. It is played on a 3-by-3 grid with 8 square puzzle labeled 1 through 8 and a blank space. The player is permitted to slide puzzle horizontally or vertically into the blank space.

Algorithms

Breadth First Search(BFS)

This is the most well known graph search algorithm. For each visited node, we immediately generate its childen and store it to the queue. and we will recursively do this util the queue is empty or the end state is reached. But, it is too slow. If you increase the shuffle time, the browser will crush.

A* (Manhattan distance)

This works similarly to BFS. Instead of using a queue, we use a priority queue(MinHeap). For each state, we will assign a value to it.
